Case 1 . Top .

$y = x^2$

$y + dy = (x + dx)^2 = x^2 + 2x\cdot dx + \color{red}{(dx)^2} = x^2 + 2x\cdot dx$

$dy = 2x\cdot dx$

$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 2x$

Case 2 . Top .

$y = x^3$

$y + dy = (x + dx)^3 = x^3 + 3x^2\cdot dx + \color{red}{3x(dx)^2} + \color{red}{(dx)^3} = x^3 + 3x^2\cdot dx$

$dy = 3x^2\cdot dx$

$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2$

Generalization . Top .

$y = x^n$

$y + dy = (x + dx)^n = x^n + nx^{n-1}\cdot dx + \color{red}{...} + \color{red}{(dx)^n} = x^n + nx^{n-1}\cdot dx$

$dy = nx^{n-1}\cdot dx$

$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = nx^{n-1}$

This formula is applicable to negative and fractional powers.
